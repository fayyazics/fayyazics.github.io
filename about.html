About me

Principal Product Designer. I see leadership as ownership without ego: raise the quality of the work, the clarity of the thinking, and the confidence of the team.

1. Craft is a leadership responsibility

Design quality is not accidental. I hold a high bar for craft, systems thinking, and clarity — and I make that bar visible. I lead through critique, example, and hands-on partnership when needed. Excellence scales when it’s modeled, not just requested.

2. Context over control

Strong teams don’t need micromanagement — they need context. I align design to business strategy, customer needs, and technical realities so designers can make autonomous, informed decisions. My job is to reduce ambiguity, not create dependency.

3. Clarity creates momentum

Ambiguity slows teams down. I push for sharp problem framing, clear success metrics, and intentional trade-offs. We don’t just ship screens — we solve problems. I help teams zoom out before they zoom in.

4. Feedback is fuel

Direct, thoughtful feedback accelerates growth. I give it generously and receive it openly. Critique is about the work, never the person. Psychological safety and high standards are not opposites — they coexist.

5. Develop people, not just products

Outcomes matter, but so does trajectory. I invest in mentoring, leveling designers up in systems thinking, storytelling, and influence. A strong design org is built through people who can think independently and lead confidently.

6. Design is a business lever

Design is not decoration — it’s a strategic function. I advocate for design at the leadership table and translate design decisions into business impact. Influence is part of the craft.

Based in Los Angeles. Past: Chorus.ai, Dynamic Signal, LSVP, Localwise. Studied CS and HCI at UC Berkeley.
