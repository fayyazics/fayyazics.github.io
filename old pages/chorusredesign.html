iOS Redesign

Chorus is a conversation intelligence platform that helps sales, customer success, and marketing teams capture every meeting to deliver better business outcomes. I joined Chorus in Oct 2019 as a mobile-focused Product Designer tasked with leveling up the existing mobile app experience. Version 2.0 of the app focused on three core workflows for users on-the-go: Browsing through recordings, Listening to recordings, and Coaching on recordings.

Timeline:
Oct 2019 - Jan 2020
My Role:
Product Designer
Tools:

The Situation

Version 1.0 of the Chorus iOS app was launched in June 2019 and hadn’t gained much traction since. Why? It was a responsive version of the feature-heavy website put into an app frame. Users found many bugs and frustrations with this app experience as they attempted to review calls from the app.

Notable usability issues included difficulty finding the right recordings based on app navigation and losing audio unless the user keeps their phone awake and unlocked.

Getting Started

My first step in tackling this redesign effort was to gather what the team knows internally. The question for members of Sales, Customer Success, and Product teams was simple -- Why do we need to step up the mobile app design?

Next, I wanted to hear from app users with some activity in the past two months. We surveyed 100 users and held 30-minute sessions with 15 other users. Surveys were focused on current app usage and what users find most valuable. User sessions were looking to dig deeper to gain an understanding of use cases, workflows, and opportunities for improvement.

For our research, we targeted the two main users of Chorus: Sales Development Representatives (“Reps” for short) and Sales Managers.

Research & Personas

Key takeaways from the research showed two major themes:

  • 1) A trend in app usage outside of work hours.
  • 2) Users looking to find specific recordings OR browse to find interesting recordings.

    • We were also able to gain a strong understanding of our users needs and develop the following personas:

    'Coaches' helped to define the primary use case for Sales Managers while 'Passive Listeners' helped us to understand how Reps would consume content using the app.

    Piecing Together the App Skeleton & Navigation

    Once we identified our key success metrics and most common workflows based on users needs, we were ready to define the structure and navigation of the app. Our goal was to keep the app as slim as possible, prioritizing user needs at all times.

    A few key decisions from this step included:

  • No Bottom Menu -- In an effort to keep the app hyperfocused, we decided to not have menu navigation.
  • Modular sections within a home screen to create a browsing effect.
  • Navigation overlays to keep a user in context.

    The Outcome

    The new Chorus app was launched in June 2020, serving as a way for employees to review calls away from their home deskspaces, on a walk, or during a break.

    Simultaneously, the mobile framework and associated components that were created laid the foundation for many features that have incrementally been built upon in the app such as sharing, transcript review, call trackers, filters, and playlists.
