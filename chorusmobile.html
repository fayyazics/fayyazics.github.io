iOS Redesign

Chorus is a conversation intelligence platform that helps sales, customer success, and marketing teams capture every meeting to deliver better business outcomes. I joined Chorus in Oct 2019 as a mobile-focused Product Designer tasked with leveling up the existing mobile app experience. Version 2.0 of the app focused on three core workflows for users on-the-go: Browsing through recordings, Listening to recordings, and Coaching on recordings.

Timeline:
Oct 2019 - Jan 2020
My Role:
Product Designer
Tools:
Sketch, Abstract, Figma
Category:
iOS
The Situation

Version 1.0 of the Chorus iOS app was launched in June 2019 and hadnâ€™t gained much traction since. Why? It was a responsive version of the feature-heavy website put into an app frame. Users found many bugs and frustrations with this app experience as they attempted to review calls from the app.

Notable usability issues included difficulty finding the right recordings based on app navigation and losing audio unless the user keeps their phone awake and unlocked.

The Outcome

The new Chorus app was launched in June 2020, serving as a way for employees to review calls away from their home deskspaces, on a walk, or during a break.

Simultaneously, the mobile framework and associated components that were created laid the foundation for many features that have incrementally been built upon in the app such as sharing, transcript review, call trackers, filters, and playlists.
