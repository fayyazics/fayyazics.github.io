The Challenge

Rebrand the landing page for Hive using specified text, site navigation with links, version live chat box, images, illustrations/icons, and 2 font choices.

Research & Basic Assumptions

The first step in design is to “unpack” what we know. Since I’m rebranding for a product, I decided to learn a little more about Hive. This included reading more info on the Castle Global site, viewing the existing landing page, learning a little more about data classification, and clicking every single link from Google search until they turn purple.

A few assumptions I made were that users visiting this site would be more technically sound, looking to view features first and foremost, and have their screen brightness all the way up.

Beginning the Design Process

I began by looking at every single detail of the current landing page and how it could be made better. A couple things I noticed and wanted to change in the rebranding were the bright orange color which could be too bright for the ordinary eye, the very little information compared to the amount of scrolling required, and the “why hive?” section shown before the “capabilities” section (I feel it should be reversed— a person should learn about the product and then be persuaded to use it…not the other way around).

With a core set of elements that I wanted to change, I proceeded to sketch a basic interface.

I began on paper where I drafted my ideas freely. Once I had a basic idea of where I was headed, I continued to a digital wireframe.

Wireframe

Wireframes are useful in showing the full flow of the page without dedicating too much time to aesthetics. It allowed me to explore the main elements such as the required text, navigation links, capabilities “cards”, “why hive?”, and the footer.

Visual Design

In this stage, I explored the aesthetic design and visual choices for the page. This includes color, type, imagery, balance, and feel. The designs were made in Sketch.

For the color palette, I chose to stick with the existing colors, but use the bright orange as a complimentary color placed in front of a dark-colored background. I used two fonts-- Avenir Medium and Avenir Heavy. I thought for a while about creating different animations to replace the existing ones, but I ultimately decided that these animations were really well done, and I’d be wasting my time trying to make different sub-par animations just to be different. Lastly, I chose to remake the Hive logo, using the same hexagon and color palette of the page. These hexagons were also used in the background to depict a similar structure of a beehive as mentioned in the “Hive Overview.”

Next Steps: High-Fidelity Prototype, Testing, Iterating, & Shipping

Though the visual designs brought a nicer look to the page, they are still limited because they don’t express microinteractions, scrolling, and touch. With any design, how it “feels” is incredibly important, and static mocks cannot always express the qualities of the app.

Due to these limitations, I made a high fidelity prototype using Principle to demonstrate the interactivity with the visuals when scrolling down.

The detailed interactions are the loading screen, the capabilities "cards" raising up when the landing page is loaded, three bullet points from "Hive is..." shifting into place from right to left, and the live chat box opening/closing.

The Result

I began with some basic assumptions and minor research. Based on the existing landing page, I created a base feature set, and then began sketching, wireframing, and figuring out the interaction details. This was translated into a visual design. The visual design was then

given interactivity. The final deliverables including the source files, style guides, color palettes, font guides, button guides, exports, and note to developers were all submitted to the Castle Global Team for further review and feedback.
